Why Instagram SEO Matters

It means making your profile and posts easier to find not only on Instagram but also on Google. In July 2025, Instagram allowed public posts (posted after January 1, 2020) from creator accounts to be indexed by search engines given that the account owner is public and over the age of 18.

This is a ground breaking update as your Instagram content will now be discovered by people just by searching on Google even if they don’t use Instagram. For example, If someone searches for “easy dinner recipes” on Google, they might see Instagram Reels from food bloggers sharing quick recipes and meal ideas. So by learning how to do Instagram SEO you can ensure that your content will show up on both places.

How Instagram Ranks Content

Instagram looks at the following three things to decide which post show up first when someone searches:

Words Used in Text

If your post has the same words that match the user’s query whether in caption, hashtags or in the image then it is more likely to show up.

What the Person Likes

Instagram algorithm remembers what people like, who they follow, and what they often look at. It then shows them more of that kind of content.

How Popular the Post Is

If a post has received a lot of likes, comments and shares, Instagram algorithm thinks that this post has a lot of engagement and shows it to more people.

An Easy Guide to Instagram SEO

Do you want more people to find your Instagram page? Here’s how to do it:

  1. Make Your Profile Easy to Find

    Your Instagram profile is the first thing people see when they search for anything. Make it clear and easy to understand.

    Username

    Add a word about what you do. Example: @FitnessWithEmma (for a fitness coach).

    Name Section

    Write your name, then what you do. Example: Emma Smith | Fitness Coach

    Bio

    Use words that explain your work. Example: “solo travel tips, budget trips, travel guides.”

  2. Turn On Search Settings

    To show your posts on Google or Bing, change your settings:

    Steps

    Go to Profile → Tap 3 lines → Account Privacy → Turn on “Allow public photos and videos to appear in search engine results.”

  3. Use Keywords in the Right Places

    Keywords are the words people search for. Use them in these areas:

    Captions

    Use 1–3 important keywords naturally in your captions.

    Reels & Carousels

    Add keywords as text on videos or images.

    Alt Text

    Add descriptions when posting, so Instagram and search engines understand your content.

    Hashtags

    Use 3–5 relevant hashtags that fit your topic.

  4. Find the Best Keywords

    Discover what people are searching for with these methods:

    Instagram Search Bar

    Type a word like “yoga” and check suggestions Instagram gives you.

    Competitors

    See which keywords popular accounts in your niche are using.

    Other Apps

    Look at TikTok, YouTube, or Pinterest for trending keyword ideas.

    Google Tools

    Use Google Trends to get fresh keyword ideas and demand data.

  5. Update the Old Posts

    Boost older posts by adding stronger keywords:

    Steps

    Go to your old post → Tap 3 dots → Edit → Update caption & alt text with keywords.

  6. Build Topical Authority

    Focus your page on one main theme so Instagram understands your niche.

    Example (Fitness)

    Create content about home workouts, bodyweight exercises, and beginner workout plans.

  7. Boost Engagement

    The more likes, comments, and shares you get, the higher your post ranks:

    Post Timing

    Post when your audience is most active (around 3 p.m. or 6 p.m.).

    Reply

    Respond to comments quickly to keep the conversation going.

    Conversation Content

    Make posts that people want to discuss and share.

  8. Consider Meta Verified

    A blue checkmark can improve trust and visibility, especially if your name is common.

    Note

    It costs money, so decide if it’s worth the investment for your brand.

  9. Add Location Tags (If Relevant)

    When posting about cafés, events, or cities, use location tags for better local reach.

    Tip

    Location tags help nearby users discover your content faster.

How to Know If Your Instagram SEO Is Working

  • Reach & Impressions: Check how many people saw your posts in search or Explore.
  • Likes, Comments, Shares: More engagement means better performance.
  • Profile Visits: Track how often users visit after seeing your posts.
  • Google Search Console: See if your posts appear on Google and what search terms bring traffic.

To check if your posts are showing up on Google, you can use a free tool called Google Search Console.
It shows which posts appear in Google searches and what words people used to find them.
